Costa Rica

Pura Vida Living & Booming Property

Market Snapshot

🏠
Foreigners have full ownership rights

Unlike many countries, foreigners can own property outright with the same rights as citizens — no special permits or local partners needed.

📈
Property values up 8-12% annually in key zones

Guanacaste, the Central Valley, and the Southern Pacific coast have seen consistent appreciation driven by expat demand and tourism infrastructure.

🌴
No property tax over 0.25%

Annual property taxes are capped at just 0.25% of the registered value — among the lowest in the Western Hemisphere.

✈️
2.5+ million tourists annually

A massive vacation rental market fuels ROI for short-term rental investors, especially near beaches and national parks.

💰
Median home price: $150K–$350K (expat zones)

Entry-level condos start around $100K, while luxury beachfront villas range from $500K to $2M+ in premium areas like Tamarindo and Manuel Antonio.

🇳🇮

Nicaragua

Central America's Hidden Gem

Market Snapshot

💵
Lowest cost of living in Central America

A couple can live comfortably on $1,500–$2,000/month. Property prices are 40-60% lower than neighboring Costa Rica.

🏠
Beachfront lots from $30K–$80K

San Juan del Sur, Popoyo, and the Emerald Coast offer oceanfront land at prices unheard of elsewhere in the region.

📜
Foreign ownership is constitutional

Nicaragua's constitution guarantees foreign property ownership rights. Transactions are recorded in a public registry system.

📍
Granada & León: Colonial gems under $200K

Beautifully restored colonial homes in UNESCO-worthy cities sell for a fraction of comparable properties in Antigua, Guatemala or Cartagena, Colombia.

🌎
Growing tourism infrastructure

New international flights, improved roads, and surf tourism are driving demand in coastal areas — creating early-mover investment opportunities.

🇦🇷

Argentina

World-Class Living at Emerging Prices

Market Snapshot

🏙️
Buenos Aires: $1,500–$2,500/m² in top neighborhoods

Palermo, Recoleta, and Belgrano offer European-style apartments at a fraction of comparable cities like Madrid, Lisbon, or Paris.

💵
All transactions in USD cash

Argentina's unique market means properties are listed and sold in US dollars — no mortgage complications and huge negotiating power for cash buyers.

📈
Rental yields of 5–8% in USD

Short-term rentals in tourist-heavy neighborhoods generate strong dollar-denominated returns, especially with platforms like Airbnb.

🍷
Mendoza wine country: vineyard estates from $200K

Argentina's Napa Valley offers boutique vineyard properties with stunning Andes views at prices that would buy a studio in California.

📋
No restrictions on foreign buyers

Foreigners can purchase property with just a tax ID (CDI). The process is straightforward with a local notary (escribano) handling the transaction.

🇨🇱

Chile

South America's Safest Bet

Market Snapshot

🏆
#1 in Latin America for economic freedom

Chile consistently ranks as the most transparent, stable, and business-friendly economy in the region — ideal for foreign investors.

🏙️
Santiago: $2,000–$3,500/m² in premium areas

Las Condes, Vitacura, and Providencia offer modern high-rises and luxury condos with world-class amenities and Andes views.

📈
Steady 4–6% annual appreciation

Chile's property market has shown consistent, sustainable growth without the boom-bust volatility seen in other Latin American markets.

📚
Foreigners can get mortgages

Unlike most of Latin America, Chilean banks offer mortgage financing to foreign buyers — typically up to 60-70% LTV with competitive rates.

🏔️
Lake District: vacation homes from $120K

Pucón, Villarrica, and Puerto Varas offer Patagonian lake houses surrounded by volcanoes and forests — Chile's answer to the Swiss Alps.
