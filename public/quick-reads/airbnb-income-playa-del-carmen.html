Airbnb Income in Playa del Carmen (2026)

By James Whitfield April 16, 2026 3 min read

Playa del Carmen is one of the most active short-term rental markets in Latin America. According to AirDNA, over 16,000 vacation rental listings operate in the city — and the market average nightly rate sits at $116 USD with a 52% occupancy rate across all listings. Here's what that actually means for your bottom line.

What a Typical Unit Earns

Market averages in Playa del Carmen vary widely depending on unit size, location, and how well the listing is managed. Here's a realistic income snapshot for 2026:

Unit Type Avg. Nightly Rate Occupancy Monthly Revenue
1-Bedroom (median) $79–$95 43–52% $1,000–$1,500
2-Bedroom (well-managed) $140–$180 65–70% $2,700–$3,800
Top 10% listings $245+ 82%+ $6,000+

The median property earns around $1,200–$1,500/month. A well-located, professionally managed 2-bedroom near Quinta Avenida or the beach can push $3,500–$3,800/month. Top performers — rooftop pools, beachfront units, or luxury condos — regularly clear $6,000/month or more.

Where Location Makes the Biggest Difference

In Playa del Carmen, location within the city has an outsized effect on Airbnb performance. Properties within 500 meters of Quinta Avenida (5th Avenue) or the beach command 30–40% higher nightly rates and significantly better occupancy than units further inland.

  • Centro / Quinta Avenida corridor: Highest demand, strongest nightly rates, best for short stays
  • Playacar: Gated community, family-oriented, strong winter/spring season occupancy
  • Colosio / Villas del Sol: Emerging area, lower acquisition cost, growing Airbnb supply

What It Costs to Buy Here

Entry-level condos in Playa del Carmen start around $120,000–$150,000 USD for a 1-bedroom. Well-located 2-bedrooms in established developments typically run $200,000–$280,000 USD. At those prices, a well-managed unit generating $2,500–$3,000/month produces a gross yield of roughly 10–15% annually before management fees and expenses.

Management fees typically run 20–25% of revenue for full-service property management, bringing net yields to approximately 7–11% — still well ahead of comparable markets in the U.S. or Europe.

The Bottom Line

Playa del Carmen is a proven short-term rental market with deep demand, year-round tourism, and relatively accessible entry prices. The median listing won't make you rich — but a well-chosen, professionally managed unit in the right location can generate income that covers carrying costs and then some, while you benefit from property appreciation in one of Mexico's fastest-growing tourist corridors.
