How Long Does It Take to Buy Property in Lisbon?

By James Whitfield April 19, 2026 3 min read

A cash buyer with a lawyer already lined up can close on a Lisbon property in as little as four weeks. With a mortgage, budget two to three months. Here is exactly what happens at each stage — and what can slow you down.

The Stage-by-Stage Timeline

Stage Typical Duration
Get your NIF & open a bank account 1–5 business days
Offer, negotiation & acceptance 1–7 days
Legal due diligence (title search, encumbrances) 5–15 business days
Sign CPCV (promissory contract, 10–30% deposit) 1–2 weeks after offer
Mortgage approval (if financing) 4–8 weeks
Final notarial deed (escritura) 4–6 weeks after CPCV

What Foreigners Need to Know

Portugal places no ownership restrictions on foreign buyers — you purchase on the same legal footing as a Portuguese citizen. Two things you must sort out before you can sign anything:

  • NIF (tax number): Obtainable at any Finanças office or via a fiscal representative. Takes one to five business days and costs almost nothing.
  • Portuguese bank account: Required to transfer funds and pay taxes. Some banks open accounts remotely; others require an in-person visit.

The CPCV (Contrato de Promessa de Compra e Venda) is Portugal's binding promissory contract — the equivalent of going "under contract" elsewhere. Once signed, backing out costs you your deposit. If the seller pulls out, they owe you double. This stage locks in the deal while due diligence and financing are finalized.

What Slows Things Down

Mortgages are the biggest variable. Portuguese banks typically lend up to 70% LTV to non-residents, and the approval process — appraisal, income verification, documentation — routinely adds four to eight weeks. Cash buyers avoid this entirely and routinely close in under six weeks from the day they make an offer.

Older properties in Alfama or Mouraria sometimes carry title irregularities that require additional legal work. New-build purchases in developments like Parque das Nações are generally cleaner but may have longer waits if the building is not yet licensed.

The Bottom Line

Buying property in Lisbon is straightforward by European standards. With a competent lawyer, a clear title, and cash in hand, four to six weeks from offer to keys is realistic. The process is more predictable than many buyers expect — the main risk is underestimating mortgage timelines or skipping proper due diligence on older stock.
