Cost of Living in Lima, Peru Per Month (2026)

By James Whitfield May 6, 2026 3 min read

Lima is South America's most underrated expat city — and one of its cheapest. According to current Numbeo data, a single expat living comfortably in a mid-tier neighbourhood spends roughly $1,400–$1,800 per month all-in. A couple can manage well on $2,200–$2,800. Here is where that money actually goes.

The Biggest Line Item: Rent

Lima's most popular expat districts — Miraflores and San Isidro — sit on dramatic Pacific cliffs and come with the price premium you'd expect. A furnished one-bedroom apartment in Miraflores runs $700–$1,000/month, with seafront or upper-floor units pushing higher. San Isidro, the business district, runs similarly.

Barranco — Lima's bohemian arts neighbourhood just south of Miraflores — offers comparable quality for $500–$750. Surco and La Molina, popular with families, come in at $450–$700. Unlike Buenos Aires or Santiago, Lima has no currency controls to navigate: the Peruvian sol is stable and most landlords are willing to price leases in either USD or soles.

Food and Dining

Lima is consistently ranked among the world's top culinary cities, and eating out is remarkably affordable. A three-course lunch at a local menú restaurant — the backbone of the Lima dining scene — costs $4–$8. Mid-range sit-down dinners in Miraflores run $15–$25 per person. Street-food snacks and anticuchos (grilled skewers) are $1–$3.

Cooking at home is even cheaper. Fresh produce at Surquillo Market — the city's best wet market — is among the least expensive in the region. Budget $150–$250/month for groceries as a single person cooking most meals.

Transport

Lima's traffic is notorious, but costs are low. An Uber ride of 5 km costs around $2.50. A monthly public transit pass (bus and the Metropolitano rapid bus) runs under $20. Most expats in Miraflores find they can walk or cycle within the district and rely on Uber for longer trips — total transport under $60–$80/month is realistic if you avoid rush hour.

Healthcare

Private healthcare in Lima is high quality and affordable. A GP consultation at a private clinic costs $25–$50; specialist visits run $50–$100. Clínica Ricardo Palma and Clínica Anglo Americana in San Isidro are the two private hospitals most expats use, with English-speaking staff. Private health insurance for a healthy adult under 55 starts at around $80–$130/month.

The Full Monthly Picture

  • Rent (1-bed, Miraflores/Barranco): $650–$1,000
  • Groceries + dining: $300–$450
  • Transport: $60–$100
  • Health insurance: $80–$130
  • Utilities (electric, water, internet): $80–$130
  • Entertainment, misc: $150–$250
  • Total (single person): $1,320–$2,060

What the Numbers Don't Capture

Peru offers one of the easiest residency pathways in South America — the Rentista visa requires demonstrating passive income of just $1,000/month. Property ownership is open to foreigners with the same rights as citizens, and there is no capital gains tax on primary residences. Lima's Pacific time zone also makes it workable for remote workers based in the US.

For investors, Lima's Miraflores rental market is tight and yields are improving as more short-term rental platforms expand in the city.
