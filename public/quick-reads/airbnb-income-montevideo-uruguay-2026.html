Airbnb Income in Montevideo, Uruguay 2026

By James Whitfield April 26, 2026 3 min read

Montevideo Airbnb hosts generated a median $7,886 annually in 2026 — roughly $825/month at the market midpoint. Top-performing properties in the top 10% clear $1,871+/month, while well-managed listings in the top 25% averaged $1,004+/month year-round. Here is what investors need to know.

The Baseline Numbers

Across Montevideo's active Airbnb market, the city-wide average occupancy runs around 67% year-round, with the median listing sitting closer to 48%. The average nightly rate is $53. Combined, a median-positioned property generates approximately $825/month gross — before Airbnb's host fee (~3%), cleaning costs, and any local management fees.

Performance tiers matter significantly here. The gap between a median listing and a well-optimized one in a prime neighborhood (Pocitos, Punta Carretas, Ciudad Vieja) is wide. Top-tier hosts leverage dynamic pricing, strong photography, and fast response times to push occupancy above 70% and ADRs to $70–$90/night — producing monthly revenues well above the city average.

Why Montevideo Holds Up Year-Round

Unlike resort markets that peak and crater seasonally, Montevideo draws a diverse demand mix: Argentine day-trippers and weekend visitors (Buenos Aires is a 2-hour ferry ride away), regional business travelers, and a growing cohort of expats and digital nomads attracted by Uruguay's political stability, dollar-denominated economy, and tax residency program. This diversified demand base supports more consistent occupancy across all 12 months — a meaningful advantage for investors who need predictable monthly income to service costs.

Punta del Este: Higher Rates, Sharper Seasonality

For comparison, Punta del Este averages $140/night — nearly three times Montevideo's nightly rate — but annual occupancy sits at roughly 46%. The December–February summer peak routinely reaches 90% occupancy, driving exceptional monthly income. The flip side: the May–October off-season requires careful cash flow management. Investors drawn to Punta del Este's headline yield numbers should underwrite on the full annual average, not peak-month performance.

What Buyers Are Paying

Residential apartments suitable for short-term rental in Pocitos, Punta Carretas, and Carrasco typically trade at $1,200–$2,200/sqm. At a $1,400/sqm entry point and median Airbnb income, the gross STR yield on a well-managed unit runs 4–6% — comparable to a long-term lease, with the upside that well-run peak periods can significantly outperform. Foreign buyers hold identical ownership rights to Uruguayan citizens under local law, with no special permits or restrictions.
