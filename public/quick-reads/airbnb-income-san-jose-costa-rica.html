Airbnb Income in San José, Costa Rica (2026)

By James Whitfield April 16, 2026 3 min read

San José is not a beach town, but it is Costa Rica's most active short-term rental market — and the numbers for 2026 are worth knowing before you invest. Here is what a typical Airbnb listing actually earns in the capital.

The Baseline Numbers

Based on current market data, a typical San José Airbnb listing generates around $11,000 per year in gross revenue. That works out to roughly $917/month — not passive retirement income, but meaningful yield on a well-priced condo acquisition.

The key metrics behind that number:

  • Median occupancy rate: 57%
  • Average daily rate (ADR): $54 USD
  • Nights booked annually: ~208

For context, the national Costa Rica average is higher — approximately $1,700/month ($20,400/year) — pulled up by premium beach properties in Guanacaste and the Southern Zone. San José trades lower nightly rates for more consistent year-round demand, since it is not a seasonal destination in the way coastal markets are.

Where the Better Returns Are

Within the San José metro, location drives a significant spread in performance. Listings in Escazú and Santa Ana — the upscale western suburbs — outperform the city centre by 20–30% on ADR due to proximity to the multinational business park corridor. Business travel and corporate relocations keep those submarkets booked through the week, not just on weekends.

Barrio Escalante and the arts district near Parque La Sabana attract a younger, longer-stay digital nomad traveler who books 7–14 night stays — reducing turnover costs and increasing effective yield even at comparable ADR.

The Tax Change to Know

Costa Rica is implementing a 12.75% tax on short-term rental income from digital platforms (Airbnb, Vrbo, etc.) in late 2026. This has been signposted for some time and is already priced into professional investors' underwriting — but it is critical to factor in if you are running projections based on gross revenue alone. Net-of-tax income on a $11,000/year listing comes to roughly $9,600.

How San José Compares as an Investment

San José is not the highest-yield Airbnb market in Latin America — El Poblado in Medellín and Playa Flamingo in Guanacaste both post stronger short-term rental returns. What San José offers instead is lower acquisition prices, an established legal framework for foreign ownership, and year-round demand that does not crater in the low season.

A well-located 1-bed condo in Escazú can be acquired for $90,000–$130,000 USD. At $11,000/year gross and roughly $9,600 net of platform taxes, that is a gross yield of 8–12% before operating costs — competitive with most developed-market urban rentals at double the price.

The Bottom Line

San José Airbnb income is modest by headline numbers, but the acquisition price is low enough that yields are respectable. The city rewards investors who pick the right submarket — Escazú and Santa Ana for business travelers, Barrio Escalante for longer-stay nomads — over those who buy generically in the centre and hope for tourist overflow.
