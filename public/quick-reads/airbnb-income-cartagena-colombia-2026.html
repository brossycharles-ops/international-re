Airbnb Income in Cartagena, Colombia 2026

By James Whitfield April 24, 2026 4 min read

Cartagena is Colombia's most-visited city and one of the Caribbean's top short-term rental markets. According to AirROI and Airbtics data, the average Cartagena Airbnb host generated roughly $16,000 in annual revenue over the past 12 months — with top-performing properties in the Walled City earning considerably more. Here is what the numbers look like for investors.

The Baseline: Rates and Occupancy

City-wide, Cartagena listings average $83–$96 per night with an occupancy rate of around 54–56%. Run the math and a typical well-managed one-bedroom apartment generates roughly $1,300–$1,600/month on a blended basis across the full year. That's before platform fees (roughly 3% on Airbnb's host side) and cleaning costs.

Top-tier properties — think colonial courtyards in the Walled City or rooftop units in Getsemaní — regularly achieve nightly rates of $130–$180 and occupancy above 65% during high season. At that level, monthly income can exceed $2,500–$3,200 in peak months.

Neighborhood Breakdown

Ciudad Amurallada (Walled City): The premium tier. Colonial architecture, walking distance to restaurants, and heavy tourist demand push ADRs 50–70% above the city average. Supply is constrained by UNESCO heritage regulations, which supports pricing.

Getsemaní: The creative-class neighborhood adjacent to the Walled City. Strong demand from younger travellers seeking boutique experiences, with ADRs of $70–$110/night. Lower entry cost than the Walled City makes the yield math compelling.

Bocagrande: Cartagena's beach-and-high-rise strip. Reliable year-round demand from domestic Colombian tourists, particularly families. Rates run $60–$90/night with occupancy slightly lower than the historic centre, but the pool of available inventory is larger.

Seasonality Is Real — Plan for It

December and January are peak months on Cartagena's Caribbean coast, with revenue running 40–60% above the annual average during the holiday window. Semana Santa (Easter week) and mid-year Colombian holidays also spike demand. Low season runs roughly May through October, when occupancy can dip to 35–45% city-wide.

Investors who underwrite on annual averages — rather than peak-month numbers — build in the appropriate buffer. A property that earns $3,000 in December and $900 in September is still a productive asset, but the blended picture is what actually determines return on investment.

What Foreign Buyers Need to Know

Colombia permits full foreign ownership of real estate with the same rights as nationals. There is no capital gains tax exemption equivalent to Panama's, but effective rates are manageable — and rental income from short-term lets is taxable as ordinary income at Colombian rates. Property acquisition costs (notary, registration, and transfer taxes) run approximately 3–5% of purchase price all-in.

Cartagena's Walled City and Getsemaní properties typically trade at $1,500–$3,000/m² in USD terms, with high-end renovated units in the historic core reaching $4,000+/m². That pricing, combined with the rental income numbers above, produces gross yields of 6–10% depending on asset quality and management.
