Airbnb Income in Mexico City (2026)

By James Whitfield May 4, 2026 3 min read

Mexico City is one of the largest short-term rental markets in Latin America. According to Airbtics, over 20,800 active listings operate across the city — and the market average nightly rate sits at $102 USD with a 70% median occupancy rate. Here's what those numbers actually mean for your bottom line.

What a Typical Unit Earns

Market performance in Mexico City varies considerably by unit size, neighborhood, and listing quality. The data below reflects 2026 performance across the full market:

Listing Tier Avg. Nightly Rate Occupancy Monthly Revenue
Median listing $85–$100 60–70% $918–$1,230
Top 25% (well-managed) $130–$160 72–80% $2,009–$2,600
Top 10% listings $200+ 82%+ $3,459+

The average host earns roughly $1,230/month — but that hides a wide spread. A median studio in Doctores won't match a well-designed 2-bedroom in Roma Norte. Location and presentation are the two biggest levers.

Where Neighborhood Makes the Biggest Difference

Mexico City's short-term rental performance is heavily concentrated in a handful of colonias. Properties in these areas consistently outperform the city average by 40–80% on nightly rate:

  • Roma Norte & Condesa: The undisputed Airbnb hotspot — tree-lined streets, walkable cafés, and a steady flow of international travelers year-round. Expect $130–$180/night for a well-appointed 1-bedroom.
  • Polanco: Upscale, corporate-adjacent, and close to major embassies. Higher price point ($150–$220/night), slightly lower occupancy but strong average revenue per night.
  • Juárez / Cuauhtémoc: Rising quickly due to nightlife and proximity to both Reforma and the Centro. Good value for buyers — lower acquisition cost with improving Airbnb demand.
  • Santa Fe / Lomas: Corporate and family-heavy; longer stays, lower nightly volatility, but thinner occupancy outside business travel periods.

What It Costs to Buy Here

Entry-level 1-bedroom apartments in Roma Norte or Condesa typically run $150,000–$230,000 USD. A 2-bedroom in the same areas will cost $250,000–$380,000 USD. At those prices, a top-quartile listing generating $2,000–$2,600/month gross delivers roughly 8–12% annual yield before expenses.

Management fees in Mexico City average 20–25% of revenue for full-service operators. Net yields for a well-chosen unit land around 6–9% — comfortably above comparable investments in the U.S., Canada, or Western Europe.

The Bottom Line

Mexico City's Airbnb market rewards specificity. The city average masks a split: median listings grind along at under $1,000/month, while properties in the right colonia, designed for travelers, and managed professionally can clear $2,000–$3,000/month consistently. If you're considering a purchase here, the neighborhood decision matters far more than the unit size.
