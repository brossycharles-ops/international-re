Cost of Living in Panama 2026: The Real Numbers

By International RE April 29, 2026 13 min read

Panama has three things no other expat destination in Latin America offers simultaneously: the US dollar as currency (zero exchange rate risk), a world-class private healthcare system, and the Pensionado visa — a permanent residency with 12 discount categories that cuts real monthly expenses by $200–600 for retirees.

It's not the cheapest country in the region. Panama City specifically has a cost floor driven by dollar pricing and a service economy that knows what expats will pay. But for retirees especially, the combination of dollar stability, healthcare quality, and Pensionado benefits makes the effective cost of living lower than the sticker price suggests.

Key advantage: Panama uses the US dollar. There is no currency conversion, no exchange rate risk, no parallel market, no inflation eroding your purchasing power. What costs $1,800/month today costs roughly $1,800/month next year. This is unique in Latin America.

Rent by Region

El Cangrejo / Bella Vista (Panama City)

$700–1,200/mo

Walkable urban neighborhoods with good local infrastructure. Close to hospitals, supermarkets, metro. Most affordable furnished expat housing in the city. Strong long-term rental supply from local landlords.

San Francisco / Miraflores

$800–1,400/mo

Mid-tier residential districts popular with young expats and professionals. Good restaurant scene, walkable in parts, reasonable commute to the financial center. Newer building stock than El Cangrejo.

Punta Pacifica / Costa del Este

$1,200–2,500/mo

Premium Panama City real estate. High-rise towers, ocean views, JW Marriott hospital adjacency. Most expensive residential area — prices approach Miami in some buildings. Best for buyers and renters wanting a full luxury urban experience.

Boquete (Chiriquí Highlands)

$600–1,000/mo

Cool mountain town at 1,200m altitude. Constant 18–22°C — no A/C needed. North America's largest expat retirement community per capita. Coffee farms, hiking, excellent produce. 45-min drive to David for airport/hospitals.

Azuero Peninsula

$400–800/mo

Traditional Panamanian heartland — Pedasi, Los Santos, Chitré. Cheapest expat living in Panama, strong surfing and fishing culture, authentic local life. Underdeveloped expat infrastructure compared to City or Boquete.

Bocas del Toro (Caribbean)

$500–1,000/mo

Caribbean island archipelago. Beautiful but infrastructure-limited — ferry to mainland, unreliable electricity, limited medical care. Best suited for adventurous expats comfortable with inconvenience for beauty.

In practice, a retiree in Panama City saves approximately:

  • Utilities (25% off $150/month) → $37/month
  • Medical visits (20% off $200/month) → $40/month
  • Medications (10% off $100/month) → $10/month
  • Flights (25% off 2 round trips/year) → $75–200/month equivalent
  • Entertainment (50% off $80/month) → $40/month

Total Pensionado savings: $200–400/month for a typical retiree, more for those with higher healthcare usage or frequent travel.

Healthcare

Panama City has some of the best private hospitals in Latin America. Johns Hopkins Medicine International manages Punta Pacifica Hospital. Cleveland Clinic has a partnership with Hospital Nacional. These are not regional approximations — they are world-class facilities at a fraction of US costs.

A/C is not optional in Panama City. The capital sits at sea level in a tropical climate — humidity and heat make A/C a necessity 10–11 months per year. Electricity bills of $120–200/month are normal for a 1BR apartment. Pensionado holders get 25% off — reducing this to $90–150/month. Boquete's highland climate eliminates this cost entirely.

FAQ: Cost of Living in Panama

How much does it cost to live in Panama per month?

$1,800–2,600/month for comfortable single-person living in Panama City. Budget living in Boquete or the Azuero Peninsula is possible from $1,400/month. Premium Panama City lifestyle runs $4,000–5,500/month. Pensionado visa holders typically save $200–400/month through the discount program.

How much is rent in Panama City in 2026?

Furnished 1BR: El Cangrejo $700–1,200/month, San Francisco $800–1,400/month, Punta Pacifica $1,200–2,500/month. Boquete furnished 1BR $600–1,000/month. Azuero Peninsula $400–800/month.

Is Panama City expensive to live in?

Most expensive city in Central America, but significantly cheaper than the US with USD pricing (no currency risk). Comparable to San José, Costa Rica. Healthcare and domestic services are affordable; luxury neighborhoods and imported goods approach US prices.

How much do Pensionado discounts save per month?

Typically $200–400/month for an average retiree. More for those with higher healthcare usage. The 25% utility discount saves $30–80/month, 20% medical discount saves $40–100/month, 25% airline discount saves $75–200/month equivalent if you travel regularly.
