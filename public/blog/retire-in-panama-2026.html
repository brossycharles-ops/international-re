Retire in Panama 2026: Pensionado Visa, Discounts & Best Places

April 29, 2026 · 12 min read · By International RE Research

Panama has built its reputation as a retirement destination on one extraordinary offer: a permanent residency visa with no renewal requirement, a USD economy that eliminates currency risk, and a legally mandated discount program that cuts your monthly costs by $200–500 across 12 categories. For retirees who want stability, modern infrastructure, and a genuine financial advantage, Panama is hard to beat.

This guide covers everything you need: the Pensionado visa requirements, the full discount breakdown, the best places to live, and what retirement actually costs in Panama in 2026.

Panama's single biggest advantage: The Pensionado visa is permanent — once granted, you never renew it. Compare that to Costa Rica (renewal every 2 years) and Colombia (renewal every 3 years). Combined with USD economy and JHM-affiliated hospital in Panama City, Panama offers the most "set it and forget it" retirement infrastructure in Latin America.

No minimum age: Panama's Pensionado visa has no age minimum — if you have $1,000/month in lifetime pension income at age 45, you qualify. This makes it attractive for early retirees with pension income from military service, government employment, or structured annuities. A Panamanian immigration attorney is strongly recommended — the process involves notarized and apostilled documents that are easy to get wrong.

Best Places to Retire in Panama

Capital City

Panama City

$1,800–3,500/mo (single)

Modern skyscrapers, world-class hospitals (Punta Pacífica / JHM), direct US flights, Tocumen hub for travel, luxury shopping. Best infrastructure in Central America. Hot year-round.

Highland

Boquete

$1,200–2,200/mo (single)

Cool mountain town at 3,900 ft — 65–75°F year-round. Large North American expat community, coffee farms, cloud forest. 45 min from David airport (flights to Panama City). Most popular non-city option.

Pacific Beach

Coronado / Río Hato

$1,400–2,500/mo (single)

Pacific Riviera, 80 km from Panama City. Weekend beach lifestyle for city-based retirees. Growing residential development, golf courses, shopping. 1.5 hrs from PTY.

Island

Bocas del Toro

$1,000–1,800/mo (single)

Caribbean island archipelago. Laid-back, boat culture, excellent snorkeling. Cheapest option in Panama. Limited infrastructure — medical care requires helicopter or ferry to David.

Pacific South

Pedasí / Azuero Peninsula

$1,100–2,000/mo (single)

Traditional Panamanian culture, whale watching, artisanal festivals. Very authentic — small English-speaking community. Emerging as boutique retirement destination. 4 hrs from Panama City.

Second City

David (Chiriquí Province)

$1,000–1,800/mo (single)

Gateway to Boquete and western Panama. Lower costs than Panama City, good local hospital, small airport. Practical base for those who want city amenities without capital city prices.

Healthcare in Panama for Retirees

Panama City has the best private hospital infrastructure in Central America. Hospital Punta Pacífica is affiliated with Johns Hopkins Medicine International — one of only a handful of JHM-affiliated hospitals outside the US. Hospital Nacional and Centro Médico Paitilla also have strong reputations with English-speaking staff.

How much money do you need to retire in Panama?

Panama's Pensionado visa requires $1,000/month in lifetime pension income. A comfortable single-person retirement in Panama City runs $2,000–3,000/month after Pensionado discounts. A couple lives well for $2,800–4,200/month. Boquete is significantly cheaper — singles can live comfortably for $1,200–1,800/month. The Pensionado discount program cuts effective costs by $200–500/month vs. the headline prices.

What is the Panama Pensionado visa?

Panama's Pensionado visa is a permanent lifetime residency visa for foreign retirees with $1,000/month in pension income. Unlike most Latin American retirement visas, it never requires renewal. It grants access to a legally mandated 12-category discount program covering healthcare, restaurants, hotels, entertainment, and utilities. No minimum age — early retirees with pension income qualify. Application requires a Panamanian immigration attorney and takes 3–6 months.

What discounts do Pensionado visa holders get in Panama?

Panama Pensionado holders receive legally mandated discounts: 50% off entertainment, 30% off hotels (Mon–Thu), 25% off restaurants (Mon–Thu) and airline tickets, 20% off healthcare, prescriptions, and utilities, 15% off hospital bills and dental, and 10% off pharmacies. Real-world savings typically run $200–500/month depending on lifestyle. These discounts are permanent and legally enforceable.

Is Panama City safe for retirees?

Panama City is safe in established expat neighborhoods — Punta Pacífica, Costa del Este, Clayton, Albrook, El Cangrejo. These areas have large expat populations, good security infrastructure, and reliable police presence. Panama consistently ranks among Central America's safest countries. Standard urban precautions apply. Boquete and Coronado beach communities have excellent safety records with very low crime rates.
