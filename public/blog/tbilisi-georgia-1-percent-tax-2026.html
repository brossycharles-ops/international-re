Tbilisi in 2026: The 1% Tax Loophole Quietly Drawing Global Investors

By James Whitfield April 25, 2026 9 min read

For most of our readers, Georgia means a peach-flavored US state, not a Caucasian republic of 3.7 million people wedged between the Black Sea and the Caspian. That obscurity is, by 2026, the single most valuable feature of its real estate market. Tbilisi delivers a combination that does not exist anywhere else in the world: instant freehold ownership for foreigners, double-digit rental yields, a 1% effective tax rate on rental income, no annual property tax under roughly $40,000 in real estate value, and residency by ownership — all in a fully convertible currency at price points starting around $800 per square meter.

Why Almost No One Talks About It

Three reasons. First, the country shares a name with a US state, which collapses search intent and floods Google with the wrong results. Second, the 2008 war with Russia and the 2022 wave of Russian and Ukrainian capital arrivals created a noise floor that obscured signal. Third, English-language broker presence is thin — the market is brokered locally, in Georgian and Russian, by small agencies without slick international websites.

This is the structural inefficiency. Markets that are hard to discover are markets that haven't yet been arbitraged.

The 1% Tax Architecture

Georgia's "small business" status is the centrepiece. An individual registered as a sole proprietor with annual turnover under 500,000 GEL (≈ $185,000 USD at 2026 rates) pays a flat 1% tax on gross turnover. Rental income qualifies. There is no separate income tax, no social tax obligation tied to the rental activity, and no requirement to be a Georgian citizen — foreign individuals can register through a Georgian tax representative.

The math: a $200,000 apartment renting for $1,400/month grosses $16,800/year. Federal tax owed: $168. Annual property tax on residential real estate valued under 100,000 GEL (≈ $37,000) is zero. Above that, residential property tax tops out at 1% of cadastral (registered) value, which is typically 30–60% of market value. In practice, holding costs on a sub-$200K property run under $500/year, all-in.

What You Actually Pay Per Square Meter

Tbilisi's residential market segments cleanly:

  • Saburtalo — the workhorse middle-class district, new builds at $900–$1,300/m². Strong long-term rental demand from Tbilisi State University and corporate tenants.
  • Vake — established prestige neighborhood near Vake Park. $1,300–$1,800/m² for renovated, $1,800–$2,500/m² for new luxury builds. Best location for premium long-stay tenants.
  • Old Town (Kala / Avlabari) — UNESCO-adjacent, the heart of short-term rental demand. Smaller floor plates, $1,400–$2,200/m². STR yields are the highest in the city, often 11–14% gross.
  • Vera & Mtatsminda — bohemian, walkable, increasingly expat-occupied. $1,200–$1,800/m². Rising rapidly as Old Town saturates.

Compare that to Tirana, Albania ($1,400–$2,000/m²), Belgrade, Serbia ($2,500–$3,500/m²), or Yerevan, Armenia ($1,800–$2,800/m²). Tbilisi is the cheapest convertible-currency capital in this entire belt, and the only one with the 1% tax structure.

Yield Reality Check

Long-term rentals in Vake and Saburtalo deliver gross yields of 8.5–10%. Tenants are mix of local professionals, embassy staff, and the post-2022 cohort of Russian/Belarusian/Ukrainian remote workers who relocated and stayed.

Short-term rentals in the Old Town and Vera deliver 11–14% gross in season, with seasonality concentrated April–October. Net yields after platform fees, cleaning, and the 1% tax typically land in the 9–11% range — extraordinary numbers for a country with EU candidate status and freely convertible currency.

Foreign Ownership Mechanics

Georgia is one of the few countries where a non-resident foreigner can walk into the Public Service Hall, present a passport, and complete a property purchase the same day. There is no nationality restriction on residential real estate. (Agricultural land has restrictions on foreign ownership; residential and commercial do not.)

The transaction process is genuinely fast: title search via the Public Registry, signed sale-purchase agreement, immediate registration, title issued in 1–4 working days. Closing costs total roughly 1.5–2% all-in — among the lowest in any market we cover. There is no transfer tax on residential.

Residency by Ownership

Purchase real estate valued at $100,000 USD or more (cadastral value, not market) and you qualify for an annually renewable residence permit. Hold for six consecutive years and you're eligible for permanent residency. Georgia is also visa-free for 95+ nationalities for 365 days at a stretch — meaning even without a residence permit, US, UK, EU, Canadian, Australian, and most other passport holders can live in country for a full year before needing to step out.

Currency & Capital Mobility

The Georgian lari (GEL) trades freely. There are no capital controls. USD-denominated bank accounts are standard at TBC Bank and Bank of Georgia, both of which list on the London Stock Exchange. Property transactions are typically conducted in USD even though title is registered in lari at the official rate. Rental income can be received in USD, EUR, or GEL. Repatriation of sale proceeds is unrestricted and untaxed for foreign individual owners holding longer than two years.

The Risks — Honest Version

Three real risks. Geopolitical: Georgia borders Russia, and the 2008 war is recent memory. The 20% of Georgian territory occupied by Russian forces (Abkhazia, South Ossetia) is far from Tbilisi but the existential reality matters. Political: 2024 saw significant unrest around the parliamentary elections and EU accession trajectory. The political path is non-linear. Liquidity: sale times in 2026 average 90–150 days for properties priced at market. A 5–10% discount to comparable sales typically clears inventory.

None of these are deal-breakers for a portfolio allocation. All of them are why the prices are what they are.

Who This Market Is For

Tbilisi is not a beach. It is not for buyers seeking lifestyle warmth or family resort assets. It is for cash-flow investors, tax-optimisers, second-passport-track buyers, and anyone willing to spend a few days in a remarkable city to deploy capital efficiently. The combination of yield, tax structure, freehold availability, and residency optionality is mathematically unique. We expect that to change as the market is discovered. We don't expect it to change in 2026.
