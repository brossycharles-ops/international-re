Santiago vs. The Lake District: Where Should You Buy in Chile?

By Sofia Mendez March 17, 2026 8 min read

Chile offers something rare in Latin American real estate: stability. The strongest economy in the region, the most transparent property market, and — uniquely — mortgage financing available to foreigners. But within Chile, two very different investment stories are playing out. Here's how they compare.

The Case for Santiago

Santiago is a modern, cosmopolitan capital of 7+ million people, backed by Chile's GDP growth forecast of 2.2% for 2026. It's safe, well-connected, and increasingly attractive to international investors — foreign investment in Santiago real estate is expected to increase 25% by 2026.

Pricing

Santiago's premium neighborhoods offer modern high-rises with Andes views at prices that would be laughable in comparable cities:

  • Vitacura: $3,000–$4,000/sqm — the most expensive neighborhood, embassy district
  • Las Condes: $2,500–$3,500/sqm — business district, excellent infrastructure
  • Providencia: $2,300–$3,000/sqm — walkable, metro-connected, popular with young professionals

For context: comparable neighborhoods in Mexico City run $3,500–$5,000/sqm. Miami: $6,000–$10,000/sqm. Santiago is still underpriced relative to its fundamentals.

Rental Yields

Santiago averages 4.91% gross rental yield, with a city-wide range of 3.84%–5.85%. Net yields after expenses land around 2.5%–3.5%. Not spectacular, but consistent and low-risk — this is a steady compounder, not a boom-bust market.

The Advantage: Liquidity

Santiago's biggest edge is liquidity. With 100,000+ unsold residential units on the market nationally, buyers have negotiating leverage. But more importantly, when you want to sell, Santiago has an active resale market with established agencies, MLS-style listings, and a deep pool of buyers. In smaller markets, selling can take years.

The Case for the Lake District

Now here's where it gets interesting. Chile's southern Lake District — anchored by towns like Pucon, Puerto Varas, and Villarrica — recorded 14.63% year-over-year price growth for new apartments in Q3 2025. That's the highest appreciation rate in the entire country.

Pricing

Lake District properties start at a fraction of Santiago prices:

  • Vacation homes: From $120,000
  • Lakefront properties: $200,000–$500,000
  • Luxury lodges/estates: $500,000+

The region is often called "Chile's answer to the Swiss Alps" — volcanoes, pristine lakes, ancient forests, hot springs. It's attracting a growing stream of North American and European lifestyle investors who want a second home in one of the most beautiful landscapes on earth.

The Advantage: Appreciation

At nearly 15% annual appreciation, the Lake District is significantly outpacing Santiago. A $10 billion coastal development project in Puerto Varas is adding institutional-grade infrastructure. The region is transitioning from a domestic vacation market to an international destination — and prices are re-rating accordingly.

The Tradeoff: Seasonality

The Lake District is seasonal. Summer (December–March) drives the bulk of rental demand. Winter brings skiing but lower overall occupancy. If you're buying for rental income, expect strong returns for 4–5 months and quieter periods the rest of the year. Santiago generates year-round rental demand.

Buying as a Foreigner in Chile

Chile makes it easy:

  • No foreign ownership restrictions — just need a RUT (tax ID number)
  • Mortgage financing available: ~4.1% average rate, 20% down for locals, 30%+ down for foreigners
  • 20–30 year mortgage terms with stricter income verification for non-residents
  • Closing costs: approximately 3–5% of purchase price

The fact that foreigners can get mortgage financing in Chile is a genuine differentiator. In most Latin American markets, it's cash-only.

Income-focused investors should lean Santiago. Steady yields, year-round demand, easy exit.

Growth-focused investors (or those who want a personal-use property in a stunning location) should look south. The appreciation story is compelling, and the infrastructure investment suggests it's not a flash in the pan.

The best play for many buyers? Both. A Santiago apartment generating steady rental income, plus a Lake District property that appreciates while you enjoy it a few months a year. Chile's mortgage availability makes splitting capital between two properties more feasible than in cash-only markets.

Sofia Mendez

Latin America real estate analyst covering Costa Rica and Chile. Based between San Jose and Santiago.
