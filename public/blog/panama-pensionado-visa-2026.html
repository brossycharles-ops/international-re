Panama Pensionado Visa 2026: Requirements, Benefits & How to Apply

April 29, 2026  ·  10 min read  ·  International RE

The Panama Pensionado visa has been called the best retirement visa in the world — and after reviewing the field, that claim holds up. $1,000/month in pension income, permanent residency from day one, and discounts of 20–50% on healthcare, hotels, flights, and restaurants. No other country comes close on the combination of low bar and high perks.

Here is the complete 2026 guide: what it is, exactly what you need, the full discount list, the step-by-step process, costs, and the property angle that most retirement visa guides skip entirely.

What Is the Panama Pensionado Visa?

The Pensionado visa (Law 6 of 1987, amended 2012) is a permanent residency visa for retirees receiving a lifetime pension from a government, corporation, or approved annuity. It is not a temporary visa — it grants permanent residency from the date of approval, with no renewals required.

Key facts at a glance:

  • Income requirement: $1,000 USD/month minimum pension
  • Residency type: Permanent (not temporary)
  • Path to citizenship: Eligible after 5 years of residency
  • Work restriction: Cannot work for Panamanian employers without a separate work permit
  • Property ownership: Full rights — same as Panamanian citizens
  • Tax: Panama is territorial — foreign-source income (including pensions) is not taxed in Panama

Requirements

✓
Valid passport — at least 6 months validity beyond intended stay. All pages photocopied.
✓
Proof of pension income — $1,000 USD/month minimum
A letter from the pension authority (Social Security Administration, pension fund, annuity company) confirming: your name, monthly pension amount in USD, that payments are lifetime/permanent, and signed by an authorized official. Must be apostilled and translated to Spanish if not issued in Spanish.
✓
Criminal background check — from your home country and any country you've lived in for the past 2 years. Must be apostilled and translated to Spanish.
✓
Health certificate — issued by a licensed Panamanian physician confirming you are free of contagious diseases. This is done in Panama (easy, takes 1-2 days, costs ~$50–100).
✓
6 passport photos — white background, recent.
✓
Marriage certificate (if including spouse) — must also be apostilled and translated.
✓
Panamanian attorney representation — legally required. You cannot file the Pensionado visa application yourself; it must be filed through a licensed Panamanian immigration attorney.

Important: "Pension income" is strictly defined. It must come from a government pension, corporate defined-benefit pension, Social Security, or a permanent lifetime annuity. Income from 401(k) withdrawals, IRA distributions, investment portfolios, rental income, or self-employment does not qualify for the Pensionado visa specifically — though other Panama visa categories (Friendly Nations, Qualified Investor) can accommodate those income types.

Step-by-Step Application Process

1
Hire a Panamanian immigration attorney — required by law. Budget $1,500–3,500 for full representation. Get referrals from expat forums (Expats in Panama Facebook group, ExpatExchange, r/Panama).
2
Gather and apostille documents — pension letter, criminal background check, and marriage certificate (if applicable) all need apostilles from your home country. Allow 2–6 weeks for this step if documents are coming from the US.
3
Translate documents — all foreign-language documents must be translated to Spanish by a certified Panamanian translator. Your attorney typically coordinates this.
4
Health certificate in Panama — done after arrival in Panama. Simple physical exam at a certified clinic, takes 1-2 days, ~$50–100.
5
File with Migración — your attorney submits the complete package to the National Immigration Service (SNM). You receive a receipt confirming the application is filed.
6
Wait for provisional approval — typically 3–5 months. You receive a provisional residency carnet (ID card) valid for 2 years while the permanent card is processed.
7
Permanent residency card issued — the full permanent residency carnet is issued within the provisional period. Your attorney handles follow-up. No need to leave Panama during this process.
8
Register for discounts — with your permanent residency card, register with the SENIAF (National Secretariat for the Social and Economic Integration of Persons with Disabilities and Seniors) to get your official Pensionado discount card recognized by all participating businesses.

Do you need to be in Panama during processing? No — you can enter on a tourist visa, file the application, and travel in and out freely while it's pending, as long as you maintain your tourist stamp validity. Many applicants file, return home, and come back when the carnet is ready.

Buying Property With a Pensionado Visa

This is the part most retirement visa guides leave out. The Pensionado visa doesn't just make your lifestyle cheaper — it directly benefits your real estate investment:

50% off closing costs on home loans. If you're financing a property purchase (Panamanian banks do lend to foreign residents), the Pensionado discount cuts your closing costs in half. On a $200K purchase with standard 4% closing costs, that's $4,000 saved immediately.

Full property ownership rights. Pensionado visa holders have identical property rights to Panamanian citizens — no restrictions, no foreign ownership caps, no special permits. You can hold property in your own name or through a Panamanian corporation (S.A.).

No capital gains tax on your primary residence. Panama exempts the primary residence from capital gains tax. Rental properties are subject to 10% capital gains on the net profit upon sale.

0% property tax on first $120,000 of value. Properties valued under $120,000 are fully exempt from property tax. Above that, rates are 0.5–0.7% annually — still among the lowest in the Americas.

FAQ

What is the income requirement for the Panama Pensionado visa?

$1,000 USD/month minimum from a lifetime pension or annuity. Must be from a government pension, corporate pension, Social Security, or approved lifetime annuity — not investment income or rental income.

Does the Panama Pensionado visa give permanent residency?

Yes — permanent residency from the day it is approved. No temporary period, no renewals. One of the only visas in the world to grant permanent residency immediately.

What discounts does the Panama Pensionado visa provide?

50% off entertainment and Monday–Thursday hotel stays; 30% off Friday–Sunday hotels and public transport; 25% off airline tickets and restaurants; 20% off medical consultations; 15% off hospital bills and dental/eye care; 10% off prescriptions; 50% off mortgage closing costs; 25% off utility bills (up to $50).

Can I work in Panama on a Pensionado visa?

No. The Pensionado visa does not authorize local employment. You can receive your pension and manage personal investments, but cannot work for Panamanian employers without a separate work permit.

How long does it take to get the Panama Pensionado visa?

3–6 months from complete document submission. You receive a provisional carnet within a few weeks of filing; the permanent card follows within the provisional period.
