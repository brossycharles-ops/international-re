Panama Digital Nomad Visa 2026: Requirements, Cost & How to Apply

April 29, 2026 · 10 min read · By International RE Research

Panama's Short Stay Visa for remote workers is one of the more straightforward digital nomad visas in Latin America — but there's a real question most guides don't answer honestly: is the formal visa even worth applying for, or should you just use Panama's generous tourist entry and do an occasional border run?

This guide covers the full 2026 requirements, step-by-step application, and gives you a clear-eyed comparison of the visa vs. the tourist entry approach so you can make the right call for your situation.

Panama's unique advantage for nomads: Panama uses the US dollar, has no currency exchange friction, and has direct flights to most US cities in 3–5 hours. Panama City's infrastructure — coworking, internet, hospitals, banking — rivals any city in Latin America. It's the region's de facto business hub, which matters when you need to meet clients or take calls in EST.

Required Documents

  1. Passport — valid for at least 6 months beyond intended stay. Color copy of bio page required.

  2. Income proof — employment letter from foreign employer (on letterhead, signed, dated) OR 3–6 months of bank statements showing consistent $3,000+/month deposits OR client contracts showing ongoing income.

  3. Criminal background check — from your home country, issued within the past 3 months, apostilled. US applicants: FBI check (takes 8–12 weeks) or state-level check (faster for some states).

  4. Health insurance certificate — must explicitly state coverage in Panama. International plans (Cigna, Allianz) work if Panama is listed. Travel insurance typically does not qualify.

  5. Two passport photos — white background, 2×2 inches.

  6. Completed application form — Formulario de Solicitud de Visa, available from SNM or your attorney.

FBI background check timing: The FBI check takes 8–12 weeks by mail (faster via approved channelers like Accurate Biometrics or IdentoGO, which can return results in 3–5 weeks). Start this first — it's the longest lead-time item in the application. Once you have the FBI check, the rest of the documents can be gathered in 1–2 weeks.

The Honest Comparison: Visa vs. Tourist Entry

Tourist Visa-Free Entry

Most Nomads Choose This
  • 180 days visa-free (US, CA, EU, UK, AU)
  • $0 cost
  • No paperwork, no attorney
  • Reset with 1-day border run (Costa Rica or Colombia)
  • No official work authorization
  • Technically not authorized for long-term remote work
  • Works fine in practice for most nomads

Short Stay Visa (Formal)

Best for 12–18 Month Stays
  • 9 months, renewable once (18 months total)
  • ~$800–1,500 total (fees + attorney)
  • Official authorization to work remotely
  • No border runs required
  • Needed for lease agreements, banking
  • Required if applying for Pensionado afterward
  • $3,000/month income threshold

Bottom line: If you're staying less than 6 months, tourist entry is simpler and free. If you're planning a 12–18 month base in Panama, the Short Stay Visa is worth the paperwork for the banking access, lease signing, and peace of mind. If you earn $3,000+/month and plan to stay longer than 18 months, consider jumping straight to the Pensionado or Friendly Nations Visa for permanent residency.

Does Panama have a digital nomad visa?

Yes — Panama's Short Stay Visa for remote workers allows up to 9 months (renewable once to 18 months total) for qualifying foreign nationals earning $3,000+/month from foreign sources. It requires income proof, health insurance covering Panama, and an apostilled background check. Most nomads staying under 6 months simply use Panama's visa-free tourist entry instead.

How much income do you need for the Panama digital nomad visa?

$3,000 USD/month in foreign-source income — from a foreign employer, foreign clients, or remote business. This must be documented with employment letters, bank statements showing consistent deposits, or client contracts. The income cannot be from Panamanian companies or clients.

How long can a digital nomad stay in Panama without a visa?

Citizens of the US, Canada, EU, UK, Australia, and most developed countries can enter Panama visa-free for up to 180 days as tourists. Many nomads simply use this and do a 1-day border run to Costa Rica or Colombia to reset the clock. The formal Short Stay Visa is most useful for stays planned beyond 6 months or when you need official work authorization for banking or lease agreements.

Is Panama good for digital nomads?

Yes — Panama City is one of Latin America's top nomad bases for business-focused remote workers. USD economy, EST time zone year-round, direct US flights, excellent coworking infrastructure, and JCI-accredited hospitals. The main downsides are the heat (hot and humid year-round), higher costs than Medellín or Bogotá, and less of a "nomad culture" vibe than Mexico City or Medellín. It's best for business-oriented nomads who value stability and US time zone alignment.
