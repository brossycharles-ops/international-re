Colombia Digital Nomad Visa 2026: Requirements, Cost & How to Apply

April 29, 2026  ·  10 min read  ·  International RE

Colombia introduced its Nómada Digital (Digital Nomad) visa in 2022 and it's become one of the most practical pathways for remote workers wanting to spend extended time in Latin America. Medellín in particular has become the default destination — fiber internet, affordable living, and a community of 8,300+ digital nomads already there when you arrive.

This is the complete 2026 guide: what it is, what you need, how to apply, what it costs, and the tax question everyone gets wrong.

What Is the Colombia Digital Nomad Visa?

The Visa de Nómada Digital (M-Nómada Digital) is a category M (migrant) visa that allows foreign nationals who work remotely for non-Colombian employers or clients to live legally in Colombia for up to 2 years. It was created under Resolution 5477 of 2022 and updated in 2024.

Key facts:

  • Duration: Up to 2 years, renewable
  • Type: Category M (Migrant) — a legal residency visa, not a tourist extension
  • Work restriction: You can work remotely for foreign employers/clients only — not for Colombian companies or clients
  • Path to residency: Does not directly count toward permanent residency (you'd need to switch to a different visa category)
  • Multiple entry: Yes — you can leave and return freely during the validity period

Requirements

The requirements are straightforward but the documentation can take time to gather:

1
Valid passport — must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended stay
2
Proof of remote income — minimum $909 USD/month
The threshold is 3x Colombia's minimum wage (approximately $303 USD/month in 2026 = ~$909 total). Acceptable proof: employment contract with a foreign company, freelance contracts, recent pay stubs (3 months), bank statements showing consistent deposits, or a letter from your employer confirming salary and remote work status.
3
Health insurance valid in Colombia
Must cover the full validity period of the visa. International health insurance plans (SafetyWing, Cigna Global, Allianz) all qualify. Budget $40–150/month depending on coverage level and age.
4
Completed online visa application
Via the Colombian Cancillería portal (cancilleria.gov.co). Completed in Spanish — use a browser with auto-translate or hire an attorney.
5
Passport-style photo — digital, white background, recent
6
Application fee payment — ~$52 USD (processing) + ~$177 USD if approved (issuance)

What you do NOT need: a Colombian bank account (though useful), a fixed address in Colombia, or a job offer from a Colombian employer.

How to Apply: Step-by-Step

1
Gather documents — collect 3 months of pay stubs/bank statements, employment contract or client contracts, and health insurance certificate. Get any foreign documents apostilled if required.
2
Create an account on the Colombian Cancillería portal at cancilleria.gov.co. The system is in Spanish — use a translator or hire a visa attorney.
3
Submit the online application — fill in personal details, select visa type M - Nómada Digital, upload all required documents as PDF/JPG, pay the $52 processing fee by credit card.
4
Wait for processing — typically 3–10 business days. The Cancillería may request additional documents (requerimiento). Respond promptly within the deadline given.
5
Pay the issuance fee — if approved, pay the ~$177 USD issuance fee. The visa sticker or electronic visa is then issued.
6
Register with Migración Colombia — within 15 days of entering Colombia on your new visa, you must register with Migración Colombia and obtain a Cédula de Extranjería (foreigner ID card). Cost: ~$30 USD.

Tip: Apply while you're still outside Colombia or well before your tourist visa expires. Do not overstay your tourist entry stamp while waiting for the digital nomad visa to be approved.

The Tax Question Everyone Gets Wrong

This is the most common point of confusion. Here's the clear answer:

Under 183 days in Colombia per calendar year: You are a non-resident for tax purposes. Income earned from foreign sources is not subject to Colombian income tax. You only pay tax on income from Colombian sources (which, as a digital nomad working for foreign clients, is likely zero).

Over 183 days in Colombia per calendar year: You become a Colombian tax resident. Colombian tax residents are taxed on worldwide income at progressive rates up to 39%. This is the trap — if you live in Medellín for a full year on the digital nomad visa, you may owe Colombian taxes on your entire remote income.

Practical approach most nomads use: Stay under 183 days in Colombia per year, or consult a Colombian tax attorney (cost: $200–500) to structure your stay properly. Many people alternate between Colombia and another country to stay compliant.

Colombia does not have a tax treaty with the United States, so you can't credit Colombian taxes paid against US taxes. This matters if you exceed the 183-day threshold.

Why Most Nomads Choose Medellín

You can use the digital nomad visa anywhere in Colombia, but 70%+ of foreign nomads end up in Medellín. The reasons:

  • Climate: 72–78°F year-round at 5,000 ft elevation. No extreme heat, no A/C required. Called "City of Eternal Spring" for good reason.
  • Cost: $1,200–1,600/month for a comfortable lifestyle including nice apartment, restaurants, and activities.
  • Infrastructure: Metro system, cable cars, fiber internet (100–500 Mbps), new airport terminal, functioning city services.
  • Community: 8,300+ digital nomads monthly. Selina, Outsite, WeWork, and dozens of independent co-working spaces. Regular meetups.
  • Neighborhoods: El Poblado (most popular, most expensive, most English-spoken), Laureles (better value, more local, excellent restaurants), Envigado (quieter, families, slightly cheaper).
  • Real estate investment upside: If you decide to buy, apartments in El Poblado and Laureles are still trading at $1,300–1,700/sqm — some of the best value in Latin America for city property.

FAQ

What is the income requirement for the Colombia digital nomad visa?

Approximately $909 USD per month (3x the Colombian minimum wage). Verified through 3 months of bank statements, pay stubs, or employment contracts.

How long is the Colombia digital nomad visa valid?

Up to 2 years, renewable. It's a Category M (migrant) visa — a real legal residency, not a tourist extension.

Can I buy property in Colombia on a digital nomad visa?

Yes. Foreigners can buy Colombian real estate regardless of visa status. The digital nomad visa makes it easier to open a local bank account for transactions.

How much does the Colombia digital nomad visa cost?

~$229 USD in government fees ($52 processing + $177 issuance). Add $300–600 for an attorney if you want assisted help.
