1. Executive summary

Latin American residential real estate is on track to grow from $687B in 2024 to over $1.27T by 2033 (6.4% CAGR). Foreign-buyer rights are equal to citizens in the four largest expat markets (Costa Rica, Panama, Colombia, Nicaragua). USD purchasing power remains historically strong, especially in Argentina and Uruguay, where 2024–25 inflation cycles compressed local-currency listings.

Top 3 picks for 2026 by use case

Highest yield: Nicaragua (San Juan del Sur) — 9–13% gross. Lowest entry price in Central America at $30K–$80K beachfront lots.

Best appreciation: Colombia (Medellín) — condo prices rose 20% YoY in 2025; US migration up 102% Q1 2025.

Best stability: Costa Rica (Guanacaste) — 8–12% annual appreciation, 0.25% property tax cap, full foreign ownership.

3. Market-by-market detail

🇨🇷 Costa Rica

Entry-level expat condos start around $100K; luxury beachfront $500K–$2M+. Top zones: Guanacaste, Tamarindo, Manuel Antonio. Rental yield strongest near national parks and Liberia airport corridor. Foreign buyers have equal rights to citizens; no permits required. Property tax capped at 0.25%.

🇵🇦 Panama

Fully dollarized economy — zero FX risk. Yields up to 9.2% in Condado del Rey. Friendly Nations Visa offers tax residency for property buyers above $200K. Strong banking sector, US-style title insurance available.

🇨🇴 Colombia

Medellín condo prices up 20% YoY in 2025; US migration up 102% Q1 2025. El Poblado and Laureles are core expat zones. Foreigner ownership unrestricted. Yields strongest on long-stay corporate furnished rentals.

🇲🇽 Mexico

Beachfront within 50km of coast requires fideicomiso (bank trust) — straightforward for foreigners. Playa del Carmen yields 8–11% on Airbnb, San Miguel de Allende strong appreciation. Closing costs 4–7%.

🇦🇷 Argentina

Primarily transacted in USD cash. Buenos Aires (Palermo, Recoleta) at $1,500–$3,500/m², still 40–50% below 2018 peak in real USD. Mendoza wine-country estates from $300K. No mortgage market for foreigners — cash deals close fast.

🇨🇱 Chile

Most transparent and stable market in South America. Santiago supply squeeze in 2026 driving rental rates. Foreign ownership unrestricted; UF-indexed contracts protect against inflation.

🇳🇮 Nicaragua

Lowest cost of entry in Central America. San Juan del Sur beachfront lots from $30K–$80K. Constitution guarantees equal foreign ownership. Best for high-yield Airbnb plays — careful counsel essential for due diligence.

🇺🇾 Uruguay

Punta del Este and Montevideo. Stable, USD-friendly, high quality of life. Lower yields but strong wealth-preservation play. Tax residency easily obtained.

🇪🇨 Ecuador

Fully dollarized — zero FX risk. Cuenca colonial center is a top expat destination. Property tax 0.5%. Yields strongest on furnished long-stay rentals.

🇵🇪 Peru

Lima (Miraflores, San Isidro) is the institutional-grade market. Entry from $1,200/m². Coastal Mancora hot for vacation rentals. Closing costs ~5%.

🇧🇷 Brazil

São Paulo and Rio for urban; Florianópolis and Pipa for coastal. Foreign ownership permitted with CPF (tax ID). BRL volatility offers re-entry opportunities at cyclical lows.

4. Tax & cost-of-ownership comparison

Annual property tax across the 11 markets ranges from 0.25% (Costa Rica) to 1.25% (Argentina). Closing costs typically 3–8% of purchase price. Capital gains treatment varies — Panama, Costa Rica and Colombia offer favorable long-hold treatment; Mexico, Brazil and Argentina apply progressive capital-gains rates.

5. Methodology & sources

Data assembled from country-specific 2026 datasets: TheLatinvestor (price-per-m² and yield bands), Panama Equity (Panama-specific listings), 2costaricarealestate.com (Costa Rica market reports), Properstar (cross-country comparisons), and central-bank data for tax and FX. Yield ranges represent gross rental yield in expat-favored zones; net yield will be lower after vacancy, management and tax.
