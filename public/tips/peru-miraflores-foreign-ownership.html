Legal Tip: Peru's Constitution Grants Foreigners Full Property Rights — Miraflores at $3,200/sqm

By International RE April 30, 2026 2 min read

Peru's constitution explicitly guarantees foreign buyers the same property rights as Peruvian citizens. No bank trust. No special permits. No workarounds. Foreigners buy directly in their own name — and in Lima's Miraflores district, those rights apply to condos priced at roughly $3,200 per square meter.

Why This Is Unusual

Most of Latin America puts friction between foreign buyers and direct ownership. Mexico requires a fideicomiso — a bank trust — for any property within 50km of the coast. Other markets have registration hurdles or capital controls. Peru is different: Article 71 of its constitution puts foreign and domestic buyers on identical legal footing.

The practical result: you close like a local. Same notary process, same title registry, same legal protections. No annual trust fees, no bank intermediary.

The Numbers in Miraflores

  • Average price: ~$3,200/sqm as of Q1 2026 — vs. $12,000+ in Miami Beach, $15,000+ in Santa Monica
  • Gross rental yields: ~6% on long-term leases in prime Miraflores
  • Rental income tax: flat 5% — one of the lowest rates in Latin America
  • Closing costs: 3–5% of purchase price
  • Foreign ownership: Zero restrictions, constitutionally guaranteed

Miraflores sits on Pacific clifftops with walkable ocean access, a dense restaurant scene, and one of South America's safest urban cores. A quality two-bedroom in the neighborhood rents for $700–$900/month and is priced under $200,000 USD to buy.

The Bottom Line

If you want oceanfront Latin American real estate without the legal gymnastics — and without paying Miami prices — Lima's Miraflores deserves 30 minutes of your research time. The constitutional ownership guarantee alone separates Peru from most of its neighbors.
