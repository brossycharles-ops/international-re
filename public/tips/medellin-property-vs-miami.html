Market Stat: Medellín Condos Cost $1,200/sqm — 93% Less Than Miami

By International RE April 15, 2026 2 min read

The average modern condo in Medellín, Colombia sells for around $1,200 per square meter. In Miami, that same square meter costs north of $16,000. Same year-round spring climate. A fraction of the price.

Why Investors Are Paying Attention

Medellín's transformation over the past decade has been dramatic. Once written off, it's now routinely ranked among Latin America's most livable cities — and foreign buyers have noticed. Neighborhoods like El Poblado and Laureles are seeing consistent demand from expats, digital nomads, and retirees priced out of coastal Florida and Southern California.

The fundamentals are compelling:

  • Entry prices: $80,000–$200,000 USD buys a quality 1–2 bedroom in a sought-after neighborhood
  • Rental yields: Short-term rentals in El Poblado routinely return 6–9% gross annually
  • Foreign ownership: No restrictions — foreigners buy in their own name with full title
  • Cost of living: 60–70% cheaper than major US cities

Colombia's peso has weakened in recent years, giving dollar-denominated buyers even more purchasing power. That window won't stay open forever.

The Bottom Line

If Miami prices have priced you out of your real estate goals, Medellín is worth 30 minutes of research. The math is hard to argue with.
