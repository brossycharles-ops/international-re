Market Stat: Dominican Republic Pulled $5B in FDI in 2025 — Real Estate Took 15.7%

By International RE April 18, 2026 2 min read

In 2025, the Dominican Republic attracted $5.03 billion in Foreign Direct Investment — a 97% jump and the fourth consecutive year of breaking its own record. Real estate captured 15.7% of that total, or roughly $790 million flowing directly into property.

Why This Number Matters

FDI figures are one of the cleanest signals of institutional confidence in a market. When capital from outside a country keeps accelerating year after year — and real estate keeps claiming a growing share of it — that tells you something that no single listing price can: the underlying market is structurally attractive to serious money.

For individual buyers, that kind of sustained inflow typically means:

  • Infrastructure spending: Roads, airports, and utilities follow big capital — Punta Cana's international airport is already one of the busiest in the Caribbean
  • Liquidity: Markets with active institutional interest are easier to exit when you're ready to sell
  • Price support: Demand from multiple buyer pools (expats, investors, tourism operators) puts a floor under values
  • Developer pipeline: New supply tends to be higher quality when competing for international capital

Entry prices in areas like Las Terrenas and Cap Cana remain accessible — quality condos in tourist zones often start under $150,000 USD — while gross rental yields on short-term platforms run 7–10% in peak coastal zones.

The Bottom Line

Four straight FDI records is not an accident. When nearly $800 million poured into Dominican real estate in a single year, it confirms what on-the-ground buyers have been reporting: this market has real momentum behind it.
