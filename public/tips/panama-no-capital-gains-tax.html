Panama Has Zero Capital Gains Tax on Property Sales — and Condos Cost 3x Less Than Miami

By International RE April 17, 2026 2 min read

When you sell real estate in Panama, individuals pay zero capital gains tax. Only a transfer tax applies — and that's it. Stack that on top of condos priced 3–5x below Miami and gross rental yields averaging 7–9% in the best neighborhoods, and Panama starts looking like a very deliberate choice.

The Tax Advantage in Plain English

Most countries hit property investors hard when they sell. The US federal long-term capital gains rate runs 15–20% on top of state taxes. Colombia charges up to 15%. In Panama, individual sellers owe no capital gains tax on the profit from a real estate sale — full stop. That alone changes the math on exit strategy.

Add two more structural advantages:

  • USD economy: Panama uses the US dollar — zero currency risk for American buyers, no exchange rate exposure eating into returns
  • Foreign buyer rights: Foreigners have the same ownership rights as Panamanians — no nationality quotas, no special approvals, full title in your own name

How the Numbers Stack Up

Mid-range Panama City condos in sought-after neighborhoods like San Francisco, Bella Vista, and El Cangrejo sell for $1,900–$3,400 per square meter. Miami's citywide median runs $6,000–$7,500/sqm. Manhattan is closer to $10,000+.

On the rental side, Panama City gross yields average 7.2% citywide — with smaller units in neighborhoods like El Cangrejo and Condado del Rey pushing 8–9%. Rents jumped 17% in 2025 while purchase prices rose just 0.8%, widening the yield gap further in buyers' favor.

The Bottom Line

Panama checks boxes that are hard to find together anywhere else: dollar-denominated, no capital gains tax on exit, full foreign ownership rights, and entry prices a fraction of comparable US markets. It's not a secret — but it's still underpriced relative to what those fundamentals would command in any other jurisdiction.
