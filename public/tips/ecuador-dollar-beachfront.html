Market Stat: Ecuador Runs on US Dollars — Beachfront Property From $800/sqm, Zero Currency Risk

By International RE April 25, 2026 2 min read

Ecuador has used the US dollar as its official currency since 2000. That means every property listing, every contract, every rental payment — all in USD. Coastal properties start at $800 per square meter. There is no exchange rate to watch and no currency risk to hedge.

The One Risk Most Investors Forget

When American buyers purchase real estate in most of Latin America, they take on a hidden second bet: the local currency. A property that appreciates 15% in Colombian pesos or Brazilian reais can still lose value in dollar terms if the currency weakens. That risk disappears entirely in Ecuador.

Ecuador is the only major real estate market in South America where transactions are conducted in actual US dollars — not a pegged equivalent, not a dollar-linked instrument. The dollar is the currency.

The Numbers

  • Coastal/beachfront properties: $800–$1,200/sqm in towns like Manta, Canoa, and Salinas
  • Cuenca (top expat city): $850–$1,500/sqm for new construction condos
  • Quito (capital): $1,300–$1,600/sqm in desirable neighborhoods
  • Investor visa threshold: $48,200 in qualifying investment — one of the lowest in the region
  • Foreign ownership: No restrictions — foreigners buy freehold, in their own name

The Bottom Line

Most investors chasing yield in Latin America are making two bets simultaneously: on the property and on the currency. Ecuador lets you make just one. At $800–$1,200/sqm for Pacific beachfront, you're paying a fraction of what comparable coastal property costs in Florida or California — without the currency complexity that comes with every other market in the region.
