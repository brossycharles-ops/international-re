Market Stat: Florianópolis Airbnb Yields Hit 8–12% — Brazil's Top Vacation Rental Market at $2,300/sqm

By International RE April 28, 2026 2 min read

Florianópolis — Brazil's subtropical island capital — is the country's #1 Airbnb market, with short-term rental yields of 8–12% annually. The average condo costs around $2,290 per square meter. Miami Beach runs north of $12,000. Foreigners buy with zero restrictions.

Why Florianópolis Stands Out

Known as "Ilha da Magia" (the Magic Island), Florianópolis has 42 beaches, a year-round tourism economy, and a growing tech and startup scene that keeps rental demand high even outside peak season. Constrained supply — you can't build infinitely on an island — has pushed prices up 8% year-over-year in nominal terms while keeping vacancy rates low.

The numbers that matter to investors:

  • Average city-wide price: ~$2,290/sqm (January 2026) — beachfront premium areas reach $3,700/sqm
  • Airbnb yields: 8–12% gross annually in tourist neighborhoods like Jurerê Internacional and Lagoa da Conceição
  • Entry point: A quality 1–2 bedroom can be had for $130,000–$250,000 USD
  • Foreign ownership: No restrictions on urban property — foreigners buy in their own name with full title
  • Appreciation: Prices rose ~8% nominally over the past year; island land supply ensures continued pressure

What Foreigners Need to Know

Brazil is one of Latin America's most straightforward markets for foreign buyers. You need a Brazilian tax ID (CPF) — obtainable in a single visit to a federal revenue office — and a local bank account to receive rents. Non-resident foreign owners pay a flat 15% withholding tax on rental income, with no deductions. Factor that into your yield math, and the net return is still well ahead of most comparable US beach markets.

One caveat: verify building rules before buying for short-term rentals. Brazil's courts have allowed condominium associations to restrict Airbnb-style rentals within their buildings, so confirm the rules before you sign.

The Bottom Line

If you're chasing vacation rental income, Florianópolis offers a rare combination: a world-class beach destination, a constrained supply market, and entry prices that haven't yet priced out serious investors. The window is narrowing — but it's still open.
